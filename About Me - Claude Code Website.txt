BIO 1
I come from a family of four children. I've lived in 11 different homes. These homes were in many places - from Iran to Switzerland to five cities in the GTA. I’ve called everything home, from cramped apartments to the dream Canadian home. 


When I was a kid, I worked with my dad in our family jewelry business. By age 15, I could spot good-quality silver jewelry from across the room. For over 20 years, we talked about business every night at dinner. We discussed new products and better sales. We talked about great customer service, building trust, and taking care of our family.


After graduating from university and backpacking through Europe, I faced a choice that kept me awake at night. Stay in the safety of what I knew - the family business that had shaped me - or step into the unknown world of corporate sales. The transition felt like moving countries all over again. I was starting from scratch, learning a completely new landscape of sales, proving myself to people who didn't know my story. Some days I wondered if I'd made a huge mistake.


Five years in corporate sales taught me something unexpected - it left me feeling empty. I liked helping people and families more than anything else. I wasn't just good at selling products; I was passionate about helping people. When I found real estate, everything clicked. This career was perfect for me. It used my people skills from our family business. It used my planning skills from corporate work. And I really understood when families need more space.


With over 10 years of experience, I now work with millennial families in Vellore Village who are bursting at the seams in their townhomes. I get it. I've lived through the chaos of not having enough space - the storage Tetris, kids arguing over bedrooms, the noise, the feeling cramped when you need room to breathe. My clients aren't just buying bigger houses. They're investing in family harmony and peace of mind. They finally get space for everyone to relax and be themselves. And after moving 11 times myself, I know exactly how to make that transition smooth.
